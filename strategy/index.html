The two channels

Channel A

Conventional, front door

Apply through the ATS with a resume tuned to the specific role.

  • A parametric builder outputs any tailored variant in one command, gated to one page
  • Never claims a skill I cannot defend in a screen; the honest gap is named, not hidden
  • Volume plus keyword match, at near-zero marginal effort per role
Channel B

Cold outbound, via Apollo

Straight to the decision-maker, not the applicant pile.

  • Every email opens with one specific, researched fact about them
  • Then a live demo link: I did not just apply, I built you something
  • Sequenced and tracked, capped at two touches, sent only to verified addresses

The wedge

"Real estate firms want underwriting-literate AI builders. They just do not post the job."

They hire onsite, or through network, not through remote listings. So those become outbound-only: a direct pitch to the CIO or CTO, where an intro is worth more than any application.
